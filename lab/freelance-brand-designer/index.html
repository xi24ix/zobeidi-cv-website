Freelance brand designer: how to choose one

As a creative agency owner or marketing manager based in the UK, finding the right freelance brand designer can be a daunting task. The success of your projects and the reputation of your agency depend on selecting a designer who not only possesses the necessary skills but also understands your industry and can adapt to your specific needs.

In this article, I will guide you through the essential factors to consider when choosing a freelance brand designer, ensuring a successful collaboration and outstanding results.

10 things to consider when selecting a freelance brand designer to work with your agency or business

1. Portfolio

A strong portfolio is a testament to a designer’s creativity and expertise. Look for designers who have worked on diverse projects and have a range of styles that align with your agency’s aesthetic and target audience. For example, if you specialise in fashion brands, you’ll want a designer who has experience creating visually stunning and stylish designs for similar clients. Request to see samples of their previous work and assess whether their design approach matches your agency’s requirements.

2. Character

Collaboration is key in any design project. Seek a designer who communicates effectively, listens attentively, and shows a genuine interest in your goals. Their passion and dedication will greatly impact the outcome. Look for testimonials or client feedback that highlights their professionalism, responsiveness, and ability to understand and translate client vision into compelling designs. For instance, a designer who actively engages in conversations, asks insightful questions, and demonstrates a keen understanding of your brand values and objectives can be a valuable asset.

3. Experience

While talent is crucial, experience brings added value. Look for designers with a proven track record in the industry, particularly in your specific niche. They will have a deeper understanding of market trends and consumer behaviour. For example, if you work primarily with technology startups, a designer who has previously collaborated with similar companies will be familiar with the industry’s unique challenges and design requirements. Consider their years of experience, client testimonials, and the brands they have worked with to assess their level of expertise.

4. Speed

Timelines are often tight in the creative industry, so it’s essential to find a designer who can work efficiently without compromising quality. Look for testimonials or references that speak to their ability to meet deadlines. Request information about their typical turnaround times for projects of similar scope and complexity. A designer who can consistently deliver high-quality work within specified timelines demonstrates professionalism and reliability.

5. Cost

Budget considerations are unavoidable. However, it’s important to strike a balance between cost and quality. Consider the value that a designer brings to your projects rather than solely focusing on the price tag. Remember, investing in a skilled and experienced designer who can effectively communicate your brand’s message and resonate with your target audience will yield long-term benefits. Ask for detailed pricing information and compare it with the level of expertise and quality of work provided.

6. Availability

Communication and accessibility are crucial for a smooth collaboration. Ensure the designer has the availability to accommodate your project and can provide timely updates and responses to your queries. Discuss their preferred communication channels and availability for meetings or calls. A designer who is responsive, proactive, and maintains clear lines of communication will help ensure a seamless workflow and project success. Bear in mind that highly talented brand designers are often in high demand. Therefore, you may need to wait a little while to get them onboard.

7. Location

While working remotely is increasingly common, proximity can still be advantageous for face-to-face meetings or to better understand local market dynamics. Consider whether the designer’s location aligns with your preferences. For example, if your agency caters primarily to local businesses, having a designer who is familiar with the local culture and preferences can provide valuable insights for your projects. However, technology advancements have made remote collaboration highly effective, so location may be less of a priority if other factors align with your agency’s requirements.

8. Adaptability

Every brand has its unique requirements. Look for a designer who can adapt their style and approach to meet your specific needs and project goals. Flexibility is key to delivering successful design solutions. A designer who can understand your brand’s vision and tailor their creative approach accordingly will ensure that the final designs align with your brand identity. Request examples of projects where the designer had to adapt to different brand guidelines or target audiences. This will demonstrate their ability to accommodate diverse design requirements and produce effective outcomes.

9. Skillset and Versatility

A well-rounded designer possesses a diverse skillset and can handle various design elements such as logos, typography, and visuals. They should also be comfortable working across different platforms and media. Look for designers who can demonstrate proficiency in various design tools and techniques. For instance, if your agency specialises in both print and digital design, seek a designer who has experience in both areas and can seamlessly transition between them. This versatility will ensure consistency across different marketing channels and deliver a cohesive brand experience.

10. Industry Knowledge

Look for a designer who understands your industry, its challenges, and the latest trends. Their industry knowledge will help them create designs that resonate with your target audience and effectively communicate your brand message. Inquire about their familiarity with your specific industry and their approach to staying updated on industry developments. A designer who shows a genuine interest in your industry and can provide insights and recommendations based on their knowledge will be a valuable asset. Ask for examples of projects they’ve worked on within your industry to gauge their level of understanding and expertise.

Final thoughts on choosing a freelance brand designer

When selecting a freelance brand designer, it’s crucial to assess their skillset. By carefully considering the factors listed above and conducting thorough discussions and assessments, you can make an informed decision that leads to a successful and productive collaboration.
